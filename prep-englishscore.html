📝 Core Skills Test — Structure

3 sections, adaptive difficulty (better you perform, harder questions get). Cannot go back to previous questions.

1
Grammar & Vocabulary
15–20 min

Multiple-choice questions testing verb tenses, word choice, prepositions, collocations. This section sets the difficulty level for Reading & Listening.

Choose the correct option: "By the time we arrived, the film _____ already started." (had / has / was / did)
Verb tenses are heavily tested — especially present perfect vs. past simple ("I have lived" vs. "I lived") and past perfect ("had done"). Read each option before choosing.
2
Reading
~10 min

Short passages (emails, notices, articles) followed by comprehension questions. Watch out for "pick the best title" questions — they're a known tricky type.

"Pick the best title for this passage" — or questions asking about the author's purpose, main idea, or detail retrieval.
Skim the questions FIRST, then read the text with those questions in mind. Don't re-read the whole passage for each question.
3
Listening
~10 min

Audio clips (conversations, announcements, short monologues) with multiple-choice questions. You may need to listen more than once — check if replays are allowed.

Conversations between colleagues, customer-service calls, airport announcements, short news reports.
Use earphones if possible — external speaker is OK but risks noise interference. Test your audio volume BEFORE starting. You'll see the question before/during the audio, so read it first to know what to listen for.

🎙️ Speaking Test — Structure

4 parts. Topics mix everyday life + workplace/professional settings. Scoring: pronunciation, fluency, communication. You can re-record most answers once.

1
Read Aloud
~3 min

You see a sentence on screen (8–24 words). Record yourself reading it out loud. You can try twice.

"The new employee will attend the training session scheduled for next Wednesday morning."
Read the sentence silently first, then aloud at a natural conversational pace. Don't rush. Pronounce word endings clearly (-s, -ed, -ing). Stress content words (nouns, verbs) more than function words (a, the, of).
2
Listen & Repeat
~3 min

Watch a video of someone saying a sentence (5–16 words). Record yourself repeating it exactly. You can try twice.

Video: "I usually go to the gym three times a week." → You record the same sentence.
Listen carefully to the intonation and stress pattern, not just the words. Copy the rhythm of the speaker. If you miss a word, use context to guess — don't freeze.
3
Answer Questions
~6 min

Watch a video of someone asking a question. Record your answer. Minimum 18 seconds, must answer EVERY part of the question. Two attempts allowed.

"Tell me about a time you worked in a team. What was your role, and what did you learn from it?"
Before recording, take 5 seconds to think. Structure your answer: situation → what you did → result/learning. Use simple but varied sentences. Don't memorize — speak naturally. Fill pauses with "Well...", "So...", "Let me think...".
4
Grade Others
~3 min

You listen to OTHER test-takers' Part 3 responses and answer questions about them. You can replay as many times as needed. This is required to get your own score.

Take this seriously — it's not optional. Listen for whether the response is relevant, clear, and grammatically correct. Report any audio problems or inappropriate content.

🗣️ Speaking: Useful Phrases (B2/C1)

Starting your answer

  • "Well, that's an interesting question. I'd say..."
  • "Let me think for a moment. In my experience..."
  • "From my perspective..."
  • "That reminds me of when..."

Structuring ideas

  • "First of all... / To begin with..."
  • "On top of that... / In addition..."
  • "On the other hand... / However..."
  • "As a result... / This is why..."

Giving examples

  • "For instance..." (more formal than "for example")
  • "Take [X] for example..."
  • "A good illustration of this is..."

If you need time to think

  • "That's a great question — let me think..."
  • "I haven't really considered that before, but..."
  • "It depends on the situation, but generally..."

✍️ Grammar: Common Traps

Present Perfect vs Past Simple
I lived in Lyon for 3 years (still there)
I have lived in Lyon for 3 years ✓
Past Perfect
When I arrived, she already left
When I arrived, she had already left ✓
Conditional 2 vs 3
If I would have known, I would tell you
If I had known, I would have told you ✓
Prepositions
She's good IN math / married WITH him
She's good AT math / married TO him ✓
Phrasal verbs
Look the word / Give the homework
Look UP the word / Hand IN the homework ✓
False friends (FR → EN)
Actually = actuellement / Library = librairie
Actually = "in fact" / Currently = actuellement ✓

🎯 Strategy

Don't overthink

With IELTS 7.5 you know more than you need. Trust your instinct — first answer is usually right. Second-guessing wastes time.

Audio setup matters

Use good earphones (wired preferred — no Bluetooth lag). Test volume BEFORE launching. Quiet room, door closed, phone on silent.

Speaking: go first take

You can re-record once, but most first attempts are fine. Only re-record if you really stumbled. Your natural voice scores better than a nervous "perfect" take.

Camera security

Random photos are taken during the test. Stay in frame, face fully visible, good lighting. Don't look away too long or the app may flag you.

Don't pause, don't exit

If you close the app, your test can be cancelled. Go to the bathroom BEFORE. Glass of water nearby. Complete in one sitting.

Speak at normal pace

AI + humans score you. Speak as you would with a friend — clear, natural, not too fast. Your accent is fine. Be understandable, not accent-free.
