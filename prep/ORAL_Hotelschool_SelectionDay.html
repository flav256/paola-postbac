2. Component 1 — Written Essay

Format

  • You will be given a choice of essay questions
  • Write your response in English
  • Timed — approximately 20-30 minutes
  • No dictionary allowed
  • Your essay will be discussed during the interview

Common Essay Themes

  • Why do you want to study hospitality management?
  • Describe a hospitality experience that inspired you
  • What does excellent guest experience mean to you?
  • How would you improve a hotel/restaurant you recently visited?
  • What role does sustainability play in modern hospitality?
  • Describe a challenge you overcame and what you learned

Paola's Essay Strategy

Structure (PEEL): Point → Evidence → Explain → Link back

Key stories to weave in:

  • Abu Dhabi hospitality scene: "Living in Abu Dhabi, surrounded by landmark hotels like the Emirates Palace, Saadiyat Island resorts, and innovative dining concepts, I have seen firsthand how hospitality creates experiences that bring people together across cultures."
  • Event coordination: "Coordinating three events for 230+ guests taught me that hospitality is not just logistics — it's designing an experience: choosing the right atmosphere, anticipating needs, ensuring every detail comes together."
  • Interior design interest: "My passion for interior design connects directly to guest experience — atmosphere and visual identity matter as much as service in luxury hospitality."
Do NOT: Write generic answers. Do NOT say "I like travelling" or "I like hotels." Be specific. Use numbers and real examples from your life.

"While I haven't worked in a hotel yet, I've been immersed in hospitality culture living in Abu Dhabi — a city built on world-class guest experiences. Q: Where do you see yourself in 5-10 years?
"After graduating, I want to start in operations at a luxury hotel — ideally in F&B or event management — to build a strong foundation. Within 5 years, I'd like to move into a management role where I can combine my multicultural background with my passion for guest experience design. Long-term, I'm drawn to the intersection of luxury hospitality and events — perhaps managing a boutique hotel or leading the events division of a luxury group."

During the boat party I organised, I spent hours comparing three nearly identical playlist options. I've learned to set deadlines for decisions and trust that 'good enough' details still create a great overall experience. In hospitality, speed matters alongside quality."
Q: How do you handle pressure/stress?
"During my military internship, I was put in high-pressure crisis simulation exercises. I learned to stay calm by focusing on the next immediate action rather than the whole situation. I applied this when our football tournament had a last-minute venue change — instead of panicking, I immediately started calling alternatives and we found a new venue within two hours."
Q: What do you know about sustainability in hospitality?
"Sustainability is becoming a core differentiator in luxury hospitality. Hotels like Six Senses and 1 Hotels have built their entire brand around it. The UN SDGs — especially SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption) and SDG 8 (Decent Work) — are directly relevant. In Abu Dhabi, I've seen Masdar City and the push toward sustainable tourism. I believe the future of hospitality is about creating extraordinary experiences while minimising environmental impact — and Hotelschool's focus on sustainability aligns with that vision."
Q: Tell us about a time you showed leadership
"When organising the boat party, we had a team of 6 students but no clear structure. I proposed dividing responsibilities — I took venue and budget, assigned social media to someone with design skills, and created a shared tracking sheet. When we disagreed on the ticket price, I facilitated a compromise by showing the budget breakdown. We sold out in 3 days. Leadership for me is about enabling others to do their best work, not just giving directions."

4. Component 3 — Knowledge Test

Format

  • 10 true/false questions based on an article you read
  • The article is provided on the spot
  • Topic is usually related to hospitality, tourism, or business
  • Tests reading comprehension and attention to detail

Strategy

  1. Read the article carefully first — don't rush to the questions
  2. Underline key facts: numbers, names, dates, specific claims
  3. Watch for tricky wording: "always," "never," "all" are often false; "sometimes," "can," "may" are often true
  4. Go back to the text for each question — don't rely on memory
  5. If unsure, the answer is usually in the exact wording of the article

5. Component 4 — Numerical/Algebra Test

Format

  • 15 questions, no calculator allowed
  • Tests basic to intermediate mathematical ability
  • Relevant to hotel/business contexts

Topics to Review

TopicExample
PercentagesA hotel has 200 rooms. If occupancy is 75%, how many rooms are occupied?
Answer: 200 x 0.75 = 150
Percentage changeRevenue went from 50,000 to 62,000. What is the % increase?
Answer: (62,000-50,000)/50,000 x 100 = 24%
Fractions & decimalsConvert 3/8 to decimal. Answer: 0.375
RatiosStaff to guest ratio is 1:4. With 120 guests, how many staff?
Answer: 120/4 = 30
Simple equationsIf 3x + 7 = 22, what is x? Answer: x = 5
Area & volumeA conference room is 12m x 8m. What is the area?
Answer: 96 m²
Average/meanGuest ratings: 8, 7, 9, 6, 10. Average?
Answer: 40/5 = 8
Revenue calculations120 rooms at €150/night, 80% occupancy, 3 nights.
Answer: 120 x 0.8 x 150 x 3 = €43,200
Profit marginRevenue €200,000, costs €150,000. Profit margin?
Answer: (200,000-150,000)/200,000 x 100 = 25%
Currency conversion€500 at rate 1 EUR = 1.08 USD. How many USD?
Answer: 500 x 1.08 = $540
Time calculationsFlight departs 14:30, duration 7h45. Arrival time?
Answer: 22:15
VAT/taxBill €85 + 21% VAT. Total?
Answer: 85 x 1.21 = €102.85
Practice tip: Do 15 mental arithmetic problems every day this week. Focus on percentages and basic algebra. With your Maths specialty (15/20), this should be straightforward — just practice doing it without a calculator.

Quick Mental Maths Tricks

  • 10% trick: Move the decimal point. 10% of 450 = 45. So 20% = 90, 5% = 22.5
  • Multiply by 15: Multiply by 10, add half. 15 x 24 = 240 + 120 = 360
  • Fractions to %: 1/4 = 25%, 1/5 = 20%, 1/8 = 12.5%, 3/4 = 75%, 2/3 ≈ 66.7%

9. Online Selection Day — Technical Checklist

  • Laptop/computer with working camera and microphone (test beforehand)
  • Stable internet connection (prefer wired/ethernet over WiFi if possible)
  • Quiet room with neutral background, good lighting (face the window)

  • Backup plan: phone with data as hotspot if WiFi fails
  • Professional attire (full outfit, not just top half — you might need to stand up)

Professional Dress Code

Recommended: Business formal or smart business casual. Think "hotel lobby manager":
  • Blazer or smart top in a neutral colour
  • Hair neat and professional
  • Minimal, elegant jewellery
  • Light, natural makeup
  • Avoid: casual clothes, visible logos, distracting patterns

10. Day-of Timeline & Mindset

Morning of Selection Day

  1. 1 hour before: Set up workspace, test camera/mic, open link
  2. 30 min before: Review pitch (say it out loud once), review closing statement
  3. 15 min before: Take 5 deep breaths. Smile. Remember: they WANT you to succeed
  4. 5 min before: Join the call, camera on, smile ready

Mindset Tips

  • Be yourself. Authenticity is the #1 thing they evaluate
  • Show passion, not perfection. They'd rather see genuine enthusiasm than rehearsed answers
  • Use concrete examples. Never make a claim without backing it up with a real story
  • Smile and maintain eye contact (look at the camera, not the screen)
  • If you don't know an answer: "That's a great question. I haven't thought about it in that way before, but my instinct would be..." — honesty is respected
  • Ask questions back. At the end, ask something specific: "What does a typical week in the Skotel look like?" or "How does HTH support students who want to focus on luxury hospitality?"
